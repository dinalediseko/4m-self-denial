1. Historical Statement

The "Big Idea" (1948)

A long time ago, a lady named Mrs. Hazel Gomez had a brilliant idea. She thought, "What if all the young people in our church saved a little bit of money to help other students go to school?" Another leader, Mrs. Alma A. Polk (the very first Connectional YPD Director), loved the idea and made it official. In January 1948, the very first Self Denial program happened. That's over 75 years ago!

Today, it's not just about raising money; it's about showing off your talents. The program now includes Boy-Girl of the Year, Writing & Art, and Digital Fun/Virtual Arts competitions.

2. Leadership Greeting

Welcome to the Self-Denial Amplified Rollout, MPowered YPD!

Have you ever felt like a heavy door stands between you and your best self—held shut by fear, doubt, or the worry of what others think?

This year, our theme, “Unlocking Me: I Surrender” (inspired by Isaiah 22:22), reminds us that we already have the key. Self-denial isn’t just about giving things up; it’s about making room—clearing out fear, distractions, and old habits so God can do something greater in our lives.

"I will place on his shoulder the key to the house of David; what he opens no one can shut, and what he shuts no one can open." — Isaiah 22:22

Instead of treating this as a once-a-year practice, we’re embracing it as a lifestyle for everyone. Join the journey by expressing your creativity in our upcoming contest and by taking part in Unpacking the Spirit from 7–14 May, where we’ll explore what it truly means to live unlocked. The question is: are you ready to open the door?

3. Keys to Self-Denial

A Daily "No": Saying "no" when desires go against God's Word, rejecting ungodly thoughts like resentment and pride, and turning away from worldly passions.
A Daily "Yes": Praying on purpose, making choices that show the Fruit of the Spirit, and setting boundaries that protect your body as a temple.

Artistic Expressions

Participants: Mother Sunbeams (Group I) & Allen Stars (Group II)

Express the theme "Unlocking Me: I Surrender" through:

  • Painting or Drawing
  • Collage
  • Virtual or Shoe Box Diorama (must include a note card explanation)

Submission Requirements:

  • Original artwork must be scanned or photographed for upload.
  • Include a Note Card explanation.
  • Photo of the original artwork + photo of the participant in Green & White Uniform.

Musical Expressions

Participants: All Groups (I, II, III, and IV)

  • Instrumental Solo: Original solo performance (no bands).
  • Gospel Song: Original song for soloists or groups (max 5 people) .
  • Liturgical Dance: Mime, Step, or Dance teams (max 5 people).

Guidelines: Length must not exceed 5 minutes and must express the theme.

Submission: Information Sheet, MP4 video format, and photo of participants in Green & White Uniform.

Literary Expressions

Participants: Youth Auxiliary (Group III) & Young Adult Division (Group IV)

  • Essay: 3-5 typewritten pages, double-spaced, including a bibliography with min. 3 outside sources + 1 biblical reference.
  • Poem: Original presentation of at least 4 stanzas.
  • Oration: 3-5 minute speech on the contest topic; include min. 1 outside source + 1 biblical reference.

Submission: Recitations/Orations must be in MP4 format. Include a photo of the participant in Green & White Uniform

Visual Arts Expressions

Participants: Youth Auxiliary (Group III) & Young Adult Division (Group IV)

Technical Specs:
• Digital Art/Photo: 300 dpi (jpg, png, etc.); max 15MB.
• Graphic Novels: Max 8 pages; max 15MB.
• Digital Film: Max 3 minutes; max 1GB.

Submission: One submission per entry; include a photo of the participant in Green & White Uniform.

YPDCARES Community Service

Participants: Youth Auxiliary (Group III) & Young Adult Division (Group IV)

Create and implement an original community service project supporting ages 2-26 based on the theme.

Project Plan (Max 5 Pages):

  • Identify root causes through research.
  • Include a SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats).
  • Detail the Project Budget and Funding Sources.
  • Provide an Organizational Contact/Partner List.

Submission: Cover sheet, report, photo in Green & White Uniform, and up to 5 photos of the project in action.
